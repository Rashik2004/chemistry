TIDAL ENERGY

Basics Terminologies Tidal Energy

  • How Tidal Energy Works

    Tidal Stream Systems
    These systems use underwater turbines similar to wind turbines. As tidal currents flow back and forth, these turbines are placed in areas with strong tidal flows to capture kinetic energy and generate electricity.
    Tidal Barrages
    A tidal barrage is a dam-like structure built across a tidal estuary or bay. As the tide flows in and out, the barrage contains sluice gates or turbines that allow water to pass through, driving turbines to generate electricity.
  • Key Aspects of Tidal Energy

    Predictability
    Tides are highly predictable based on lunar cycles, making tidal energy a reliable and predictable source of renewable energy.
    High Energy Density
    Tidal currents are denser than wind and contain more energy, allowing for more consistent power generation.
    Low Environmental Impactidal energy has a relatively low environmental impact compared to some other energy sources. However, its installation may affect local ecosystems and marine life.
  • Advantages and Challenges

    Advantages
    Predictability and Reliability
    Tides are highly predictable based on lunar cycles, providing a consistent and reliable source of energy. This predictability allows for accurate planning and energy generation forecasts.
    Renewable and Sustainable
    Tidal energy is renewable and inexhaustible, relying on the gravitational forces of the moon and sun, which are consistent natural phenomena.
    Low Environmental Impact
    Tidal energy generation produces minimal greenhouse gas emissions, contributing to cleaner energy and a reduced carbon footprint compared to fossil fuels.
    High Energy Density
    Tidal currents are denser and contain more energy than wind or solar resources, allowing for potentially more consistent power generation.

    Challenges
    High Initial Costs: Building tidal energy infrastructure, such as barrages or turbines, can be expensive.
    Limited Locations: Suitable sites for tidal energy extraction are limited to areas with strong tidal currents.
